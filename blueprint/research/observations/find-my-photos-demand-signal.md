# Observation — the find-my-photos demand signal

**Type:** operator field observation (not an interview transcript; a recurring, directly-witnessed behavior).
**Observed by:** Nino Chavez (operator/photographer), across the grass-volleyball tournaments he shoots.
**Recorded:** 2026-06-10, during this Blueprint-port session.

## The observation

Players — the athletes Nino photographs — want to see themselves, and they will sometimes ask him **mid-match**: *"did you get that?"* — meaning *did you capture my play / my moment, and can I see it?*

This is the recurring, in-the-field demand signal that grounds the entire product. It is not a hypothesized persona: it is a behavior Nino witnesses repeatedly at the events he shoots (the Let's Pepper Open grass-triples series — Bell Pepper / Jalapeño / Poblano, Aurora IL).

## Why it is the pilot anchor

The athlete asking "did you get that?" defines the whole job-to-be-done:

1. **Discover the event** — they know *which* tournament they played (by name, date, sport), so the entry point is event discovery, not a stock-photo search.
2. **Find themselves** — they identify by **jersey number** and **team color**, never by name or face. This is exactly what the relational `photo_jersey_sightings` model serves (46,702 sightings, 26,358 with a jersey number, as of 2026-06-10) — pre-resolution signals, human-resolved, no AI-created identities.
3. **Download / share** — the payoff is retrieval, not purchase. The product is operator-funded goodwill: **no sales, no prints, no per-person naming, no face recognition.**

## What it rules OUT (scope discipline)

- It is **not** the event *organizer's* job (running the tournament, standings, brackets) — that is Rally HQ's pilot, on the same events.
- It is **not** a coach building highlight reels or managing a roster.
- It is **not** a print/merch buyer — there is no store.
